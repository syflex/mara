# A0 Resource Map

Free YouTube reference material for the A0 syllabus (`SYLLABUS_A0.md`). Two creators are mapped: **Dutchies to be — Learn Dutch with Kim** (numbered "Dutch for BEGINNERS" lesson series, A0→A1) and **Learn Dutch with Bart de Pau** (the "1000 most common words" video course + the "Heb je zin?" scenario soap + pronunciation course).

**Usage note.** Link-only. No transcripts copied. Videos are intended as references for the lesson author and optional "verder kijken" links inside the app. Both creators publish on YouTube under standard YouTube ToS; their materials are free to view but copyrighted — do not re-host clips. Bart's word lists on `learndutch.org` are free; the e-book PDF was used here only to audit vocabulary coverage.

## Vocab gap check (Bart's top 300 vs syllabus §2)

Bart's *1000 most common words* course is **strictly topic-ordered, not frequency-ordered.** The first 300 words = lessons 1–12 (verified against the official `e-book-1000-words.pdf` on `learndutch.org`). Themes in the first 300: greetings + intro + articles (L1), pronouns + yes/no/conjunctions (L2), zijn + professions (L3), food/drink (L4), question + demonstrative words + some conjunctions (L5), traffic & buildings (L6), numbers 0–million (L7), 25 high-frequency verbs (L8), adjectives (L9), days/months/seasons (L10), prepositions (L11), telling the clock (L12). Lessons 13–25 = ~325 more words covering hobbies, Amsterdam, house, body, clothes, more verbs, family, animals.

**A0 vocab present in Bart's top 300 (matches syllabus §2):**

- §2.1 Greetings: hallo, hoi, dag, goedemorgen, goedenavond, doei, alsjeblieft, alstublieft, dank je wel, ja, nee — present (L1–L2).
- §2.2 Personalia: ik, ik heet, ik ben, een, de, het, zijn, de student, de leraar — present (L1, L3). Several professions covered.
- §2.3 Numbers: nul…twintig, eenentwintig, dertig…honderd, duizend, miljoen — present (L7).
- §2.4 Time/date: full days + months + seasons (L10); full clock (L12).
- §2.8 Food/drink (core): water, koffie, thee, melk, bier, wijn, brood, kaas, vlees, aardappel, groente, fruit — present (L4).
- §2.10 Verbs: hebben, gaan, eten, drinken, zien, weten, leren, lezen, schrijven, spreken, zeggen, werken, lopen, krijgen, kopen, vragen, antwoorden, rijden, vergeten, horen, luisteren, kijken, slapen, denken, wachten — present (L8).
- §2.11 Function words: ik, jij/je, hij, zij/ze, wij/we, jullie, u + en, of, maar, want, ja, nee, niet, wel — present (L2, L5).
- §2.12 Prepositions: all 15 from §2.12 — present (L11): met, van, naar, in, voor (twice — both "in front of" and "before"), achter, naast, boven, onder, op, tussen, over, bij, tegen, om.
- §2.13 Question words: wie, wat, waar, wanneer, hoe, hoeveel, waarom, welke — present (L5).

**A0 vocab MISSING from Bart's top 300 (lives in later lessons):**

- §2.1 Greetings: goedemiddag, goedenacht, tot morgen, tot ziens (tot + ziens are L1 separately but not as one chunk), graag gedaan, sorry / pardon, meneer, mevrouw.
- §2.2 Personalia: naam (noun), heten (infinitive form — only "ik heet" is in L1), wonen, komen (in L23), oud, jaar, land, stad, straat, adres, telefoonnummer, spreken (in L8 — actually present), beetje, Nederlands, Engels, Nederland, België, nationaliteit, werk, baan (in L19), nationaliteit.
- §2.5 Family: vader, moeder, broer, zus, opa, oma, tante, oom, vriend, vriendin, kind, man, vrouw (vrouw/man are in L1) — **family is L20**, well outside top 300.
- §2.6 Body & feeling: full body parts list — **L16**, outside top 300. Feelings (moe, ziek, blij, boos) split across L9, L29.
- §2.7 Huis: huis (in L1), but kamer, woonkamer, slaapkamer, keuken, badkamer, deur, raam, tafel, stoel, bed, bank, kast, lamp, boek, pen, tas, sleutel — **L15**, outside top 300.
- §2.8 Food/drink remaining: boter, ham, ei, sap, appel, banaan, rijst, pasta, vis, soep, koken (verb), eet smakelijk, proost, lekker (lekker is L9).
- §2.9 Colors: rood, blauw, geel, groen, wit, zwart, bruin, grijs, oranje, paars, roze — **L35**, far outside top 300. (Some adjectives — groot, klein, mooi, lelijk, oud, jong, koud, warm, duur, hoog, laag — *are* in L9.)
- §2.10 Verbs remaining: willen, kunnen, moeten, mogen, doen, maken, geven, nemen, komen, praten (komen + geven + nemen + maken + doen are all in L18/L23 — outside top 300).
- §2.11 Function-word gaps in top 300: dus, ook, dit, dat (dit/dat are in L5, so present), deze, die (also L5 — present), heel, erg, een beetje, zeer, veel, weinig, alle, sommige, een paar, mijn (poss pronouns are not in L1–L12 — possessives come later).

**Net assessment.** Bart's top 300 covers the **grammatical core** of §2 well (pronouns, articles, numbers, days/months, clock, prepositions, question words, core verbs, basic adjectives) but the **thematic A0 vocab** for family, body, house, colors, full food list, and possessive pronouns lives in his lessons 15–35 — so the syllabus §2 is **NOT** a strict subset of the top 300. About 60–65 % of §2 words appear in the top 300; the other 35–40 % require lessons 15, 16, 20, 35 (still inside the free 1000 — just not the first 300).

**Implication for the syllabus TODO** ("confirm A0 vocab is in the top 300"): the answer is **no, not entirely**. If the goal is "use Bart's top 300 as A0 vocab anchor," roughly two thirds aligns and the remainder must come from Bart's later thematic lessons (15, 16, 20, 35) or from Kim's themed videos. Consider relaxing the TODO to "A0 vocab is in Bart's full 1000-words course" (which it is, completely).
