# Nederlands in Gang — A0 coverage cross-check

This document compares §2 (vocabulary) and §3 (grammar) of `SYLLABUS_A0.md` against the early chapters of **Nederlands in Gang** (NiG) — the standard Dutch-university NT2 textbook for hoogopgeleide anderstaligen (highly-educated non-native speakers) targeting CEFR A0→A2. Goal: validate that our A0 scope is well-aligned with what the leading textbook teaches at the entry tier, and surface gaps or over-reaches.

## NiG chapter map (18 chapters total; A0/A1/A2 verdict per chapter)

NiG itself is not partitioned by CEFR sub-level in the publisher's own description — the entire book targets A0→A2. The widely-used NCTaal split (and the natural grammar progression visible in the TOC) places lessons 1-9 in the A0-A1 tier and lessons 10-18 in the A1-A2 tier. Within the A0-A1 tier, lessons 1-3 are the **A0 core** (only `zijn`/`hebben` + present, no regular conjugation pattern taught yet, no inflection), and lessons 4-9 are the **A1 ramp** (regular verb conjugation, modals, plurals/adjectives, separable verbs, perfectum start, etc.). This is the same split our syllabus §3.8 implicitly adopts.

## Coverage analysis (§2 vocab + §3 grammar)

### Overlap (both NiG A0 and our syllabus)

Both NiG chapters 1-3 and SYLLABUS_A0.md cover, with very similar treatment:

- **Greetings & politeness** (§2.1) — NiG H1
- **Personal info: name, origin, residence, language, nationality, age** (§2.2) — NiG H1
- **Numbers 0-100** (§2.3) — NiG H1
- **Days, months, time of day, clock (hour + half)** (§2.4) — NiG H2
- **Family & people** (§2.5) — NiG H2
- **Food & drink (ordering basics)** (§2.8, partial) — NiG H3
- **Function words: pronouns, possessives, de/het/een, ja/nee/niet/geen, basic conjunctions** (§2.11) — NiG H1+H2+H3
- **Question words** (§2.13) — NiG H2 (wh-questions are explicit there)
- **Personal pronouns full table** (§3.1) — NiG H1
- **`zijn` & `hebben` full conjugation** (§3.2, §3.3) — NiG H1
- **de/het articles, learn-with-article principle** (§3.4) — NiG H3
- **`een` vs `geen`, `niet` placement (basic)** (§3.6) — NiG H3 (full treatment is H8 but starter usage is H3)
- **Word order: SVO statement, verb-first yes/no question, wh-question** (§3.7) — NiG H1 (statement & inversion) + H2 (question types)
- **Pronunciation foundation (alphabet, vowel length, hard sounds g/ch/ui/ij)** (§1) — NiG H1 plus running pronunciation features in every chapter (NiG has dedicated pronunciation videos per chapter on the Coutinho website)

Strong overlap. Our §2-§3 scope sits cleanly inside what NiG H1-H3 teaches.

### Only in NiG A0 (potential gaps in §2)

Items NiG covers in chapters 1-3 that our §2/§3 either omit or treat very lightly:

- **Possessive pronouns as a full taught table (H2).** Our §3.1 has them in the pronoun table, but §2 and the can-do list never explicitly drill "Dit is *mijn* broer / *jouw* zus / *zijn* huis" as a function. Recommendation: **leave in §3.1 but add to §4** as an explicit can-do ("Introduce a family member with the correct possessive — *Dit is mijn / haar / zijn …*"). It's already half-there in the §4 family-introduction bullet; just tighten the possessive requirement.
- **Yes/no question formation as an explicit topic (H2).** Our §3.7 covers it in one line. NiG dedicates a sub-section. Recommendation: **leave** — our coverage is adequate; we just don't dwell on it.
- **Seasons (lente, zomer, herfst, winter) (H2).** Not in our §2.4. Tiny gap (4 words). Recommendation: **add to §2.4** — they slot naturally next to months and pay off in weather small-talk (a very common Dutch register-opener).
- **Ordinal numbers (rangtelwoorden — eerste, tweede, derde …) (H3, used for ordering & date formation).** Not in our §2.3. NiG introduces them around H3 for ordering ("de eerste koffie") and dates. Recommendation: **add a short ordinals row to §2.3** at recognition level only (1st-10th + 20th + 30th), production stays at A1. Justification: real-world dates ("3 juni" → "de derde juni") and floor numbers (tweede verdieping) start being needed at A0 in NL.
- **Diminutives as a category (recognition, H3).** Our §3.8 explicitly defers diminutives to A1. NiG introduces them at H3 for recognition only. Recommendation: **align with NiG — flip the §3.8 entry to "recognize -je/-tje diminutives; no production"**. Cost is one paragraph; benefit is that "een koffietje" / "een biertje" become decodable, which they already are in our §2.8 scenarios.
- **Spelling/dictation drill explicitly framed (H1 pronunciation focus).** Our §1.6 has it as a milestone, but no §2 list. Fine as-is; just noting the parallel.
- **Cardinal directions / location prepositions in real scenes (H3 inversion examples often use "Het café is op de hoek").** Our §2.12 has the prepositions but no "linkout/right/straight" type spatial vocab. Recommendation: **leave** — that's an A1 directions theme (NiG H4 "Op straat"), not an A0 must.

### Only in §2 (we go beyond NiG A0)

Items in our §2/§3 that NiG either defers past H3 or doesn't teach at A0:

- **Body parts & feelings (§2.6).** NiG defers the body to H9 (huisarts). Assessment: **justified deviation** — we anchor "ziek/moe/honger/dorst/pijn" as A0 because Inburgering A2 expects fluent expression of personal state at any point, and one of our §4 can-dos is "say they're sick/tired/cold." Carrying 20 words for body+feeling at A0 is cheap and high-ROI. **Keep.**
- **House & objects (§2.7).** NiG defers home/objects to H8 (makelaar/housing) and H17 (Thuis). Assessment: **slightly over-ambitious but justified** — we cap it at ~20 high-utility nouns (huis, kamer, deur, tafel, stoel, sleutel, …) which are useful as de/het practice fodder regardless of theme. **Keep, but make explicit that these are vehicles for article practice, not a full "huishouden" theme.**
- **Colors + basic adjectives bundle (§2.9, ~25 items).** NiG defers colors to H7 (kledingzaak) and adjective inflection to H5. Assessment: **justified deviation, with a caveat** — we teach colors and uninflected adjectives at A0 but explicitly defer adjective *inflection* to A1 (§3.8). That's the right call. Just make sure the lesson sequencing introduces adjectives in predicate position only ("De auto is rood") and not attributive ("de rode auto").
- **Formal *u* register taught from day 1 (§3.1, §4 register policy).** NiG also shows *u* in H1 (it's unavoidable in greetings) but doesn't drill the formal/informal split with equal weight. Assessment: **justified deviation** — our learner is preparing for Inburgering and lives in NL, so the formal register has high payoff from week 1. NiG's profile is university students who can defer this. **Keep.**
- **Full modal verb roster (mogen, kunnen, willen, moeten) at A0 lexically (§2.10).** NiG defers modals to H6 (restaurant). Our §3.8 explicitly says modals are A0 lexically but not in full sentences. Assessment: **justified** — we need "ik wil koffie" / "ik moet gaan" as frozen phrases for §5 scenarios. **Keep, with the discipline that we don't teach the full modal grammar pattern.**
- **Pronunciation-as-system §1 (alphabet + vowel length + digraphs + stress).** NiG teaches pronunciation feature-by-feature across chapters via dedicated videos, never as one consolidated A0 module. Assessment: **justified deviation** — front-loading pronunciation is a deliberate A0 strategy in our syllabus (see §1 intro), grounded in the fact that adult learners benefit from explicit phonological orientation early. **Keep.**

## Net assessment

Our §2-§3 scope is **well-aligned with what NiG teaches across its A0 cluster (chapters 1-3) and, by deliberate choice, also pulls forward a few high-ROI A1 themes** (body/feeling, house objects, colors as predicates, modals lexically) that pay off in Inburgering-specific scenarios. There is **no major theme NiG teaches at A0 that we omit**; the only true gap is a small one — seasons and ordinal numbers — both of which can be added with ~10 words total. The biggest structural difference is that **NiG sequences vocabulary by scenario** (kantine → café → markt) while our syllabus **sequences by grammar load**; both are legitimate and neither is "wrong." Where we differ from NiG philosophically is in the §3.1 register decision: we teach *u* and *je* in parallel from day 1, while NiG is informal-first. Given the Inburgering target, this is the right call.

**Concrete changes to tighten alignment (in priority order):**

1. **Fix the source citation in `SYLLABUS_A0.md` line 26**: "Burger & Doef" → "Berna de Boer, Margaret van der Kamp & Birgit Lijmbach". The "Burger & Doef" attribution does not match the actual textbook (and may be confusion with a different NT2 method).
2. **Add to §2.4** the four seasons (lente, zomer, herfst, winter) as recognition vocab. ~4 words.
3. **Add a short recognition-only ordinal row to §2.3** (eerste–tiende, twintigste, dertigste) so dates and floor numbers are decodable. Production stays at A1. ~12 items.
4. **Flip the diminutive entry in §3.8** from "deferred to A1" to "recognition only at A0; production at A1." This aligns with NiG's H3 treatment and matches the §2.8 reality where "een biertje" already appears in scenarios.
